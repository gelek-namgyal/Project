Mustang

Explore the rugged landscapes and rich culture of Mustang.

Mustang is a unique and remote region in Nepal. It is known for its breathtaking landscapes, high desert terrain, and rich Tibetan Buddhist culture. Visitors can explore its ancient monasteries, caves, and beautiful villages like Lo Manthang.

Pokhara

Enjoy the serene lakes and mountain views of Pokhara.

Pokhara is a popular tourist destination in Nepal, known for its picturesque lakes, beautiful mountain views, and adventure sports. Tourists can enjoy boating on Phewa Lake, paragliding, and visit places like the World Peace Pagoda and Sarangkot for stunning mountain views.

Darjeeling

Experience the breathtaking tea gardens and mountains of Darjeeling.

Darjeeling, known as the "Queen of the Hills," is famous for its beautiful tea gardens, the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, and stunning views of Mount Kanchenjunga. The town offers a cool climate and a rich blend of Indian and Tibetan cultures.

Humla

Discover the untouched beauty and culture of Humla.

Humla is one of Nepal's least-visited regions, known for its pristine natural beauty, rugged terrain, and Tibetan influence. The area is rich in culture, with unique monasteries and remote villages that are yet to be explored by many tourists.

Lumbini

Visit the birthplace of Buddha and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Lumbini is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became Buddha. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and attracts thousands of pilgrims and tourists annually. The sacred garden, ancient ruins, and various monasteries offer spiritual experiences for all visitors.

Janakpur

Explore the vibrant culture and historical temples of Janakpur.

Janakpur is known for its rich cultural heritage and the Janaki Temple, dedicated to the Hindu goddess Sita. It is a significant pilgrimage site and an important location in Hindu mythology, with vibrant festivals and local traditions.

Ilam

Enjoy the lush tea gardens and scenic beauty of Ilam.

Ilam is a beautiful hill district in eastern Nepal, known for its lush green tea gardens, serene landscapes, and cool climate. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful retreat while exploring tea estates, waterfalls, and local markets.
